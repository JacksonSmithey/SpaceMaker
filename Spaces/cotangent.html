Euclidean n-Space \(\mathbb{E}^n\)

The space you know and love. The Euclidean 2-Space is the flat plane where polygons live. The Euclidean 3-space is where polyhedra live.

The Set n copies of the real number line.

The Structure In geometry, this space is formed by a simple list of postulates (rules)

  1. A straight line segment can be drawn joining any two points.
  2. Any straight line segment can be extended indefinitely in a straight line.
  3. Given any straight lines segment, a circle can be drawn having the segment as radius and one endpoint as center.
  4. All Right Angles are congruent.
  5. If two lines are drawn which intersect a third in such a way that the sum of the inner angles on one side is less than two Right Angles, then the two lines inevitably must intersect each other on that side if extended far enough.
